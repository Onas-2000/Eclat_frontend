Different Account Types

Dated/Fixed Account

A Dated or Fixed Account allows you to deposit a lump sum for a fixed period, earning higher interest rates than regular savings accounts. This type of account is ideal for individuals looking to save for specific goals while ensuring their funds remain untouched until the maturity date. Early withdrawals may incur penalties, making it a great option for disciplined savers.

Save

Target Account

The Target Account is designed for individuals who want to save towards specific goals, such as a vacation, home purchase, or education. This account allows you to set a target amount and track your progress. With features like automatic transfers and goal notifications, it encourages saving while providing flexibility in how you achieve your financial objectives.

Save

Asusu/Ajo Savings Account

The Asusu or Ajo Savings Account is a traditional saving method where a group of individuals contribute a fixed amount regularly to a pool. At the end of a predetermined period, each member receives a lump sum, promoting collective saving and financial discipline. This account is great for communities or groups looking to save together and share resources.

Save

Eclat Group Contribution (Thrift)

The Eclat Group Contribution account, commonly known as a thrift account, is similar to the Asusu model but often includes additional features such as loan options and interest on contributions. Members contribute regularly and can access funds for emergencies or planned expenditures while benefiting from group solidarity and shared financial goals. This account promotes community support and encourages responsible saving habits.
